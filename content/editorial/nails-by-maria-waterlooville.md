## Skill Meets Care

Good nail work requires both technical skill and artistic eye. Getting the shape right, the application smooth, the design precise — it takes training and practice to do consistently well.

But beyond technique, there's the care factor. Taking time to understand what the client actually wants. Not rushing to fit in extra appointments. Checking the work meets the standard before the client leaves.

## Why Independent Matters

Chain nail bars work on volume. The pressure to turn over clients quickly means corners get cut, conversations get rushed, and the experience becomes transactional.

Independent technicians like Maria work differently. Building a client base through quality and reputation rather than foot traffic and discount offers. Each appointment matters because each client matters.

## The Details Count

In nail work, the details are everything. The cuticle prep, the product quality, the curing time, the finishing touches — cutting any of these short shows up in how long the results last and how they look day to day.

Clients who've experienced both rushed chain salon work and proper independent service know the difference immediately. It's not just about how nails look walking out — it's about how they hold up.
